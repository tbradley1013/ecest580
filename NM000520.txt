            Summary: This gene encodes a member of the glycosyl hydrolase 20
            family of proteins. The encoded preproprotein is proteolytically
            processed to generate the alpha subunit of the lysosomal enzyme
            beta-hexosaminidase. This enzyme, together with the cofactor GM2
            activator protein, catalyzes the degradation of the ganglioside
            GM2, and other molecules containing terminal N-acetyl hexosamines.
            Mutations in this gene lead to an accumulation of GM2 ganglioside
            in neurons, the underlying cause of neurodegenerative disorders
            termed the GM2 gangliosidoses, including Tay-Sachs disease
            (GM2-gangliosidosis type I). Alternative splicing results in
            multiple transcript variants, at least one of which encodes a
            preproprotein that is proteolytically processed. [provided by
            RefSeq, Jan 2016].
            
            Transcript Variant: This variant (2) uses an alternate in-frame
            splice site in the 5' coding region compared to variant 1. The
            encoded isoform (2) is shorter than isoform 1.
